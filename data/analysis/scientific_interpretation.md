Real-world water quality interpretation requires contextual environmental understanding, not just numerical analysis.

A water quality scientist considers:
- Seasonal and climatic variation
- Source of contamination (biological vs operational)
- Treatment plant conditions and operational logs
- Historical baseline data trends
- Public health implications
- Whether RPD variations are acceptable under QA/QC standards

This contextual reasoning is essential for accurate environmental decision-making and cannot be fully replaced by numeric-only AI interpretation.
